HM Service Learning

WHAT IS A SERVICE-LEARNING PROJECT?

A Service-Learning Project is an action and learning experience you undertake, alone or with others, to a) make an impact on your wider community and b) learn something important to you.  It must contain both clear service and learning components and objectives.  The project must demonstrate a sufficient level of commitment, most often including planning and collaboration with others, along with participation in an activity itself.  

We will provide opportunities throughout the year for reflection, which is another central component of service-learning.  

Please refer to the “Six Service-Learning Values” page for the guiding principles behind projects.  

We have developed a list of pre-approved projects in which you may participate and fulfill your requirement.  Some of you will want to propose something new or different, through the form on these Haiku pages.

The goal of the HM Service-Learning requirement is to help you at all stages of the process. You need not feel that you have a finished idea, a complete plan, etc. before you propose a project.  If this is a new project, please complete the form to the best of your ability and submit it to the Tenth Grade Committee.

Service activities, even some wonderful ones, may not be approved without crucial additions that make them complete service-learning projects.  Even though the work may be fanatasic, don’t risk having your hard work not count towards fulfillment of your requirement!   Submit your form and obtain approval before  you undertake the activity.  

WHAT MAKES A SUCCESSFUL PROJECT?

A successful project has a Goal , a Program/Activity , and criteria for Evaluation .  Projects will also have an adult liaison.

GOAL:   What issue does the program address? Or, What do you hope participants (including you!) will get out of the experience?

PROGRAM/ACTIVITY:   The activity should be well-planned, addressing the goal.  Most activities will take place over more than one visit/day/event.   Continuity  is an important part of service-learning.  

EVALUATION:   Please consider how you would answer these questions:

How will you know if your events/activities have been successful?  

Were there surprises or unanticipated experiences, either positive or negative (or both!)?

Do you see a possibility for next steps?  Would you like to continue in this activity?  (Please note: your comments here in no way obligate you to continue, if you decide otherwise.)

What did you learn?

What would you need to know to do more in this area?

ADULT LIAISON:   It is necessary that either a faculty member, a representative from an outside agency, or other adult “sign off” on your project.  Different projects will require different levels of adult supervision and responsibility.  Before you submit your project, please have an adult willing to oversee your experience.  If you are unable to find the right person, please contact the CCVA office.  

APPROVAL:   Please enter your project idea on the Project Proposals in the submission tab. . You can also enter your project, and get more information, through the Haiku page.  You will be contacted by the Tenth Grade Service-Learning Committee with information on the next steps.  This can include Approval; Request for more information; Request to meet with the committee.  In rare cases, the project may not be approved because it does not fit the mission of the program.  

Please think about these issues in advance of your submission of your Project Proposal online.
